About this TechLady Hackathon + Training Day

Coders, tech strategists, trainers, newbies, and ideas are welcome! Email or tweet at me for the registration link.

The goal of this hackathon + training day is for you to contribute to a project you find meaningful, learn new tech, make new friends and connections, and then attend more DC tech meetups/hackathons - especially Code for DC! We want you to walk away feeling inspired and empowered to get more involved in tech.

Saturday Dec. 7 Hackathon is for women identified folks. Email or tweet at me for the registration link.
Friday Dec. 6 pre-party at MapBox is for everyone! Sign up: techladyparty.eventbrite.com.

This hackathon is brought to you by Leah Bannon with Code for DC and Tech LadyMafia, and Kat Townsend, Jen Higgens, Shannon Turner, Sarah Rigdon, Rebecca Williams, Shauna Dillavou, Ousmane Mariko (who designed our logo), and Matt Bailey.

Training
We will offer basic 101 intro training and mentoring workshops throughout the day. Attend sessions all day or dip in and out while you contribute to a hackathon project.

Right now, we're planning to offer: Ruby, APIs, Python, JavaScript, GitHub, html/CSS, and SQL. Mentoring session ideas so far are: Empowerment/imposter syndrome, open gov/open data, and a business lady session. Ideas? Are we missing anything important? Submit issues on this GitHub repo or email.

Hackathon
We're planning to work on some awesome projects and challenges, mostly with a focus on open gov projects and helping out some cool lady small business. Please submit ideas!

Questions? Ideas? Advice?
